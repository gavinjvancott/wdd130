The AVP

What is the AVP?

The AVP, or Assosoation of Volleyball Professionals, is a huge professional beach volleyball tournament that is played in many states. The AVP has been played since 1983, and has been very succesful as an organization. These competitions are open, as long as you are a member of the AVP. The tournaments are double elimination style, in which you are not eliminated until you lose two matches. There are 16 teams for the men and the women, and the winner will win a cash prize. The championship game is played in Manhattan Beach, and has the top volleyball players in the world competing.

Where is it played?

The AVP is played in many locations around the USA, and this year alone it will be played in twelve states. The most popular location out of all of them however is Manhattan Beach, where the championships are played. Other popular locations are Miami, Hunnington, Hermosa, and Chicago.

Who Participates?

When considering who is a part of the AVP it seems like only the top professional Olympians are competing, but this is not true. The AVP has opportunities for a lot of people ranging from youth to upcoming adults. There are three main programs the AVP has first the youth tournament, second the developement program, and third the well-known professional tournament. There are three tiers to the professional tournament, the Tour Series, the Pro Series, and the Gold Series. The Gold series is the most competitive of the three and has the most prize money to play for. In order to be entered into this level tournament you must qualify for the tournament based off of your pool play.

The AVP History

The AVP has been one of the most prestigious and popular beach volleyball events since it started in 1983. There have been many different people that have dominated over the years, and there has been a huge community that is extremely close to one another. The competition has been open to people of all skill levels, but there are obvious competitors that are specifically known to be competing for the trophy and the AVP Champions title. Some popular names to note that currently play are Phil Dalhausser, Tri Bourne, Taylor Crabb, and Trevor Crabb for the mens tournament, and April Ross, Keri Walsh Jennings, and Alex Klinemen for the womens tournament. Some successful people in the past were Kent Steffes and Karch Kiraly who dominated in the 90's.
